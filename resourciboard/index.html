The first being an internet connected electronic monitoring and control device within a small enclosure called the Elbeano. This preceeded the IoT movement, and was too costly and restricted due to the non-inclusion of WiFi, and the requirement for DC power. It was based around the Arduino Pro Mini.

Read about the Elbeano

The second project being based around the ESP32-cam as an automatically uploading webcam. If left running it would freeze up at different intervals requiring a power cycle reset. This was achieved originally by way of a standard scheduled power socket adapter, but the urge to have it all integrated within the one box introduced the 555 with a relay, but with a little more work it was determined that it would be far more flexible if the 555 was swapped with a microprocessor to store wifi settings, timer settings, and the like.

Read about the initial timer board

Borrowing the design theory from the original project, with the flexibility of the camera timer, and a few years of design, code, and practical tests, and a lot of skills learned along the way, the ResourciBoard was born.

Power

Designed to demonstrate the portability and charge capabilities of the ResourciBoard.

Direct from USB

Onboard regulator to change 6.5v+ down to 5v or 3.3v

With a LI-ION 10440 battery, and the screw terminals underneath, the enclosure can stay completely weather-sealed while recharging when returned to a charging base.

Standard AAA

Onboard boost to change < 4.5v to 5v and 3.3v

Features

  • Swappable BIOS & APP chips
  • Ultra low power sleep mode
  • Two Analog inputs, three digital outputs, one digital (PWM) output
  • Regulated output voltage – 3.3v, 5v
  • Dedicated MOSFET ground switch
  • Status Led
  • IP67 rated enclosure
  • Dedicated on/off timer
  • Wake-on-change interrupt

IO

  • One digital (PWM) output
  • Two Analog inputs
  • Three digital outputs
  • I2C
  • Shift1
  • WiFi / Bluetooth
  • 9600 baud serial bus

Power sources

  • AAA Alkaline 1.5v
  • AAA LI-ION 1.5v
  • 10440 LI-ION 3.7v Rechargeable
  • 3.3v regulated
  • 5v regulated
  • >4.5v unregulated
IO pins and their abilities overlap with eachother limiting the amount of peripherals. Only one power source can be provided at one time. Battery type practicality is determined by the peripheral current requirements.
